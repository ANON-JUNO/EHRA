Introduction

Dante's Inferno

Dante's Inferno, written by Dante Alighieri in the 14th century, is an epic poem and the first part of The Divine Comedy trilogy. The poem follows Dante's journey through Hell, guided by the Roman poet Virgil.

Characters

  • Dante Alighieri: The protagonist and narrator.
  • Virgil: Dante's guide through Hell.
  • Beatrice: Dante's beloved, who awaits him in Heaven.
  • Charon: The ferryman of the dead.
  • Minos: The judge of the dead.

Circles of Hell

  • Limbo (1st Circle): Where unbaptized souls and virtuous pagans reside.
  • Lust (2nd Circle): Where those who succumbed to lust are punished.
  • Gluttony (3rd Circle): Where the gluttonous are forced to lie in a foul slush.
  • Greed (4th Circle): Where hoarders and spendthrifts push heavy weights.
  • Wrath (5th Circle): Where the wrathful are stuck in a river of boiling blood.
  • Heresy (6th Circle): Where heretics are burned in fiery tombs.
  • Violence (7th Circle): Where violent souls are punished in three rings:
    • Outer ring: Violence against others (burning in boiling blood)
    • Middle ring: Violence against oneself (suicides turned into thorny trees)
    • Inner ring: Violence against God (forced to wander in a desert of burning sand)
  • Fraud (8th Circle): Where deceivers are punished in ten bolgias (ditches).
  • Treachery (9th Circle): Where traitors are frozen in a lake of ice (Cocytus).

Summary

Dante's Inferno tells the story of Dante's journey through the nine circles of Hell, encountering various historical and mythological figures, and witnessing their punishments. Along the way, Dante confronts his own sins and moral weaknesses, ultimately emerging transformed and ready to begin his ascent towards spiritual redemption in Purgatorio.
